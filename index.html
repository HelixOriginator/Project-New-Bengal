Supreme Court Judgment — 03 April 2025

2025 INSC 437 · Civil Appeal out of SLP(C) No. 9586 of 2024 & Ors. · Bench: CJI Sanjiv Khanna & J. Sanjay Kumar

Supreme Court of India CBI Investigation ED / PMLA 2016–2025

WBSSC Recruitment Scam — En Bloc Cancellation Upheld

The Supreme Court upheld the Calcutta High Court's judgment of 22 April 2024, affirming cancellation of the entire 2016 selection process by the West Bengal Central School Service Commission (WBSSC) for approximately 24,640 posts — Assistant Teachers (Classes IX–XII) and Non-Teaching Staff (Groups C & D). The ruling rejected appeals by the State of West Bengal, WBSSC, and affected selectees.

"Manipulations and frauds on a large scale, coupled with the attempted cover-up, have dented the selection process beyond repair and partial redemption. The credibility and legitimacy of the selection are denuded." — Para 20, 2025 INSC 437, Supreme Court of India

WBSSC admitted at least 6,276 illegal appointments: 1,498 out-of-panel candidates illegally appointed, 926 involved in rank-jumping, and 4,091 recommended despite OMR score mismatches. CBI forensic analysis of hard disks recovered from an ex-NYSA employee in Ghaziabad revealed manipulation in 952 Class IX-X candidates, 907 Class XI-XII candidates, 3,481 Group C candidates, and 2,823 Group D candidates. The Board issued 2,355 excess appointment letters beyond WBSSC's own recommendations.

F-01

WBSSC destroyed physical OMR sheets (July 2019) despite ongoing recruitment process, violating applicable rules for non-teaching Groups C & D where no destruction provision existed.

F-02

CBI forensic comparison of hard disks seized from ex-NYSA employee Pankaj Bansal (Ghaziabad) with WBSSC server data revealed systematic score inflation across all four categories.

F-03

Board issued 2,355 excess appointment letters beyond WBSSC recommendations. Candidates with blank OMR sheets received appointment letters and joined schools.

F-04

WBSSC applied for supernumerary posts to retroactively accommodate illegally-appointed candidates — a State-level attempt to institutionally launder the fraud.

F-05

Court held delay and laches do not apply as fraud was concealed until 2021–22. The cover-up itself made segregation of tainted/untainted candidates impossible.

F-06

Tainted candidates ordered to refund all salaries + 12% p.a. interest. Untainted candidates' services terminated; entitled to participate in fresh selection with age relaxation.

SC Judgment — Guiding Principle on En Bloc Cancellation

"When an in-depth factual inquiry reveals systemic irregularities, such as malaise or fraud, that undermine the integrity of the entire selection process, the result should be cancelled in its entirety… The decision to cancel the selection en masse must be based on the satisfaction derived from sufficient material collected through a fair and thorough investigation. It is not necessary for the material collected to conclusively prove malpractice beyond a reasonable doubt. The standard of evidence should be reasonable certainty of systemic malaise. The probability test is applicable."
2025 INSC 437, Para 19 — Supreme Court of India, 03 April 2025

Chronological Record

Key Events Timeline: 2011–2026

A chronological map of documented governance challenges and institutional responses in West Bengal.

2011

TMC Comes to Power; Saradha Accelerates

TMC ends 34 years of Left Front rule. SEBI warns WB Government about Saradha's collective investment scheme. Depositor recruitment intensifies across rural Bengal with large agent commissions and political backing.

April 2013

Saradha Ponzi Collapse — 1.74M Depositors Affected

Sudipto Sen's 18-page confession letter alleges political pressure. Sen arrested in Kashmir. West Bengal creates SIT and Justice Shyamal Sen Commission. Multiple states initiate CBI transfers. 1.74 million depositors compile claims.

May 2014

Supreme Court Orders CBI Probe into Saradha; Narada Sting Filmed

SC mandates CBI investigation across eastern India. Narada sting operation secretly filmed showing TMC leaders allegedly accepting cash. CBI registers FIR under Prevention of Corruption Act. CBI arrests MP Srinjoy Bose (Nov 2014) and Minister Madan Mitra (Dec 2014).

2016

WBSSC Tainted Selection; Narada Videos Released Publicly

WBSSC issues notifications for ~24,640 teaching and non-teaching posts. M/s. NYSA engaged for OMR scanning — later revealed as the conduit for score manipulation. Narada footage publicly released. First writ petition filed in Calcutta HC by December 2016.

July 2019

Critical Evidence Destroyed: OMR Sheets Deleted

WBSSC Chairman orders destruction of physical OMR sheets despite ongoing recruitment process and in violation of rules for Groups C & D. Subsequent investigation reveals scanned mirror copies in WBSSC server were also deleted — a cover-up the Supreme Court termed decisive evidence of fraud.

May 2021

Post-Poll Violence; Narada Arrests; NHRC Intervenes

Post-election violence: 1,934+ NHRC complaints; suo-motu cognizance taken. Governor grants prosecution sanction; CBI arrests ministers Firhad Hakim, Subrata Mukherjee, Madan Mitra, Sovan Chatterjee in Narada case. SC intervenes in procedural disputes.

July 2022

Partha Chatterjee Arrested; ₹49 Crore+ Cash Seized

ED arrests Education Minister Partha Chatterjee. ₹21 crore cash + ₹50L jewellery from Arpita Mukherjee's Kolkata flat; second raid yields ₹27.90 crore + 6 kg gold from Belghoria apartment. Sacked from cabinet; suspended from TMC. CBI arrests SSC and Board officials.

August–October 2022

Cattle Smuggling & SSC Arrests Intensify

CBI arrests Anubrata Mondal (cattle smuggling). CBI arrests Shanti Prasad Sinha, Ashok Saha (SSC officials), Kalyanmoy Gangopadhyay, Subiresh Bhattacharya. ED arrests TMC MLA Manik Bhattacharya. Three hard disks recovered from Pankaj Bansal's Ghaziabad home reveal OMR manipulation.

2023

More Arrests; SC Re-assigns Bench

ED arrests Kuntal Ghosh, Shantanu Bandyopadhyay, Sujay Krishna Bhadra ('Kalighater Kaku'). CBI arrests MLA Jiban Krishna Saha who attempts to flee and destroys evidence. SC re-assigns SSC bench after Justice Gangopadhyay's controversial media interview.

January 2024

Sandeshkhali: ED Officials Attacked; 55-Day Evasion

5 Jan: ED team attacked during enforcement operations. Sheikh Shahjahan goes into hiding for 55 days despite warrants. Calcutta HC transfers probe to CBI. SC rejects WB Govt challenge. Land-grabbing and sexual assault allegations surface.

April 2024

Calcutta HC Sets Aside Entire WBSSC Selection

Division Bench landmark judgment declares all ~24,640 appointments null and void. Orders salary recovery at 12% interest from tainted candidates. Directs CBI to continue investigation. Mandates fresh transparent selection process.

3 April 2025

Supreme Court Upholds En Bloc Cancellation (2025 INSC 437)

CJI Sanjiv Khanna & J. Sanjay Kumar uphold HC order. Tainted candidates must refund all salaries + 12% p.a. interest. Others' services terminated but may apply for age-relaxed fresh selection. First post-poll violence rape conviction also secured in 2025.

2026

Fresh Actions: New Arrests; 59 Cases Reopened

ED arrests Sujit Bose (Municipality Scam) and Sona Pappu (Land Grab/Hawala). Kaliachak judicial hostage crisis. 59 historical cases reopened + 181 fresh FIRs. Fresh WBSSC recruitment process formally initiated per SC directive.

Core Philosophy

Why This Framework Works

Built on three pillars: documented accountability, constructive vision, and open-source collaboration for citizen-empowered governance.

Strictly Official Sources

Every case is sourced exclusively from ED, CBI, NHRC, Supreme Court, Calcutta High Court, and government records. No commentary, no speculation — only verifiable facts with direct PDF links and judgment citations like 2025 INSC 437.

Constructive, Not Adversarial

Positioned as governance diagnostics, not political attack. Organizations see it as balanced public interest work: diagnosing structural failure points using official sources while proposing the New Bengal Renaissance Model as the solution.

Open-Source & Collaborative

Hosted on GitHub with co-authorship opportunities for NGOs and think tanks. Organizations get content + DOI/Zenodo ecosystem; researcher gains publishing infrastructure. Zero funds required. Creative Commons licensed.

Dharma-Driven Governance

Rooted in the Helix Originator philosophy — independent research creating forward-looking, policy-intelligence frameworks for citizen security, institutional reform, and systemic accountability without retribution.

Co-Branded Partnerships

Target ADR, Transparency International India, CPR Delhi, CRG Kolkata, and fact-finding NGOs. Offer co-authorship credit on balanced governance research that enhances both public credibility and institutional reach.

Zero-Cost Execution

Free GitHub repo, free PDF publishing on SSRN/ResearchGate, free Amazon KDP eBook + Pothi.com print-on-demand, free Notion/GitHub Pages hosting. The only investment is rigorous, verifiable research.

Important Research Disclaimer

This framework is compiled strictly from official sources including ED press releases, CBI charge sheets, Supreme Court judgments (including 2025 INSC 437 dated 03 April 2025), Calcutta High Court orders, NHRC fact-finding reports, and government FIRs. No commentary, speculation, or unverified claims are included.

All cases listed are based on documented agency actions and court proceedings. Many involve ongoing trials where presumption of innocence applies in criminal proceedings. The purpose is governance diagnostics and institutional learning — not political advocacy or personal prosecution.

Primary sources include: sci.gov.in (Supreme Court), calcuttahighcourt.gov.in (Calcutta High Court), cbi.gov.in (CBI), enforcementdirectorate.gov.in (ED), nhrc.nic.in (NHRC), and official West Bengal Police/Government records.
